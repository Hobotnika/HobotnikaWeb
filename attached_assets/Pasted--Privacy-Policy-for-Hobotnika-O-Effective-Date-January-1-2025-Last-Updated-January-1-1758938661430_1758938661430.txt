## 6. Cookies and tracking technologies

### 6.1 Cookie Categories

We use the following categories of cookies:

**Strictly Necessary Cookies**  
Essential for website functionality, security, and fraud prevention. These cannot be disabled.

**Functional Cookies**  
Remember your preferences and enhance website features. Consent required in EU/UK.

**Analytics Cookies**  
Help us understand website usage through Google Analytics. We use Google Analytics with IP anonymization and have configured it to comply with privacy regulations. Consent required in EU/UK.

**Advertising/Marketing Cookies**  
Enable targeted advertising through Google Ads, including conversion tracking and remarketing. Used to measure advertising effectiveness and deliver relevant ads. Consent required.

### 6.2 Google Services Disclosures

**Google Analytics**  
We use Google Analytics to analyze website usage. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect and process data. We have implemented IP anonymization, disabled data sharing, and configured appropriate retention periods. Learn more about how Google uses information from sites that use their services at www.google.com/policies/privacy/partners/

**Google Ads and Remarketing**  
We use Google Ads for advertising and remarketing services. Third-party vendors, including Google, use cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to our website or other websites. Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to our sites and/or other sites on the Internet.

**Google Consent Mode v2**  
For visitors from the EEA and UK, we implement Google Consent Mode v2, which adjusts how Google tags behave based on consent status for:
- ad_storage: Controls storage of advertising-related data
- analytics_storage: Controls storage of analytics-related data
- ad_user_data: Controls collection of user data for advertising
- ad_personalization: Controls personalized advertising

### 6.3 Managing Cookies

**Opt-Out Options:**
- **Google Ads Settings:** Visit www.google.com/settings/ads to opt out of personalized advertising
- **Network Advertising Initiative:** Visit www.networkadvertising.org/managing/opt_out.asp
- **Your Online Choices:** Visit www.youronlinechoices.eu (EU) or www.aboutads.info/choices (US)
- **Google Analytics Opt-Out:** Install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
- **Browser Settings:** Configure your browser to refuse cookies (may impact website functionality)
- **Global Privacy Control:** We honor GPC signals where legally required

## 8. Your privacy rights

### 8.1 Rights Under GDPR (EU/UK Residents)

You have the right to:
- **Access** your personal data and receive a copy
- **Rectification** of inaccurate or incomplete data
- **Erasure** ("right to be forgotten") under certain circumstances
- **Restrict** processing in specific situations
- **Data Portability** to receive data in a structured format
- **Object** to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing
- **Withdraw Consent** at any time where processing is based on consent
- **Not be subject** to solely automated decision-making

### 8.2 Rights Under CCPA/CPRA (California Residents)

You have the right to:
- **Know** what personal information we collect, use, share, and sell
- **Access** your personal information (twice annually free of charge)
- **Delete** your personal information (with certain exceptions)
- **Correct** inaccurate personal information
- **Opt-Out** of sale or sharing of personal information
- **Limit** use of sensitive personal information
- **Non-Discrimination** for exercising privacy rights

**Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information:** California residents can opt out of the sale or sharing of their personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising by visiting our "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link in the website footer or by enabling Global Privacy Control in your browser.

### 8.3 Rights Under PIPEDA (Canadian Residents)

You have the right to:
- **Access** personal information we hold about you
- **Correct** inaccuracies in your information
- **Withdraw Consent** for certain processing activities
- **File a Complaint** with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner

### 8.4 Rights Under LGPD (Brazilian Residents)

You have the right to:
- **Confirmation** of processing and access to data
- **Correction** of incomplete or inaccurate data
- **Anonymization, blocking, or deletion** of unnecessary data
- **Portability** to another service provider
- **Information** about sharing with third parties
- **Revocation** of consent

### 8.5 Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@hobotnika.com. We will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law (generally within 30 days). We may request information to verify your identity before processing your request.

## 9. Data retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy:

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data, including:

**Technical Measures:**
- Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
- Multi-factor authentication for system access
- Regular security assessments and penetration testing
- Intrusion detection and prevention systems
- Secure backup and disaster recovery procedures

**Organizational Measures:**
- Staff training on data protection and security
- Access controls and role-based permissions
- Confidentiality agreements with all personnel
- Vendor due diligence and contractual protections
- Incident response and breach notification procedures

## 11. Data breach notification

In the event of a personal data breach, we will:
- Notify the Estonian Data Protection Inspectorate within 72 hours (if required)
- Inform affected individuals without undue delay if the breach poses high risk to rights and freedoms
- Document all breaches and maintain records for regulatory compliance
- Implement measures to prevent future incidents

For US residents, we comply with applicable state breach notification laws. California residents may be entitled to statutory damages in certain breach situations.
